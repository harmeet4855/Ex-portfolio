Flex Flights : Flexible Date Booking Model 

Project Description:   In-depth analysis for the need of a flexible date booking model for flights assuming dynamic flight fares. Work involved primarily quantitative research into the problems faced and the state of art, further data visualization was used for a clear visual representation of data collected from questionnaire responses and several conclusions were made based on these visualizations pointing to the need of a flexible date booking model.  

Project Status: Complete

Guide: Asst. Prof. Naveen Kashyap
Keywords:  Flights, Dynamic Fares, Booking  
Project Duration: Jan - April’15

Problem Statement 

Airline companies have a very smart policy of dynamic Pricing where the ticket price varies on parameters which the company identifies and in such a manner that is most favourable to the company in terms of profit. As a result majority of the consumers which here implies commuters prefer booking the ticket well in advance which in turn leads to an uncertainty in many cases related to the exact travel date. The same ticket when rescheduled, in case travel plans need to be modified incur upon the consumers heavy charge in comparison to the original ticket price which was booked way too early owing to the very policies of dynamic pricing and skyrocketing flight process.There needs to be some flexibility in this regard which makes the decision making process easy for the consumers, which provides a shield to the commuters against this clever dynamic pricing which leaves them helpless and which is mutually rewarding for the commuters and the flight companies so that they can survive in the cut throat coompetition they engage in with their competitors.

  

Hypothesis

An add-on service that provides the consumers some flexibility by allowing them to book the ticket for a time frame rather than one particular date would fit very well to the flight service industry. Further this add-on would be highly suitable for people who book flights well in advance but might not be that useful for those who book tickets closer to the date as in that case the commuters are mostly sure of their travel dates. As backed by the Prospect Theory , people are reluctant to experience a huge loss in a single go and are rather willing to cough out some extra cash in each purchase if there is a guarantee of being on the safer side. The insurance service industry is analogous to this.

Data Analysis

Data Visualization is the primary method used here for coming onto a conclusion. Plots of scatter and linking were done using the data obtained and coding the same to map the responses. Data visualization is based on some parameters extracted from questionairre and it gives a much clearer picture of the need for this kind of service.

 

The third level here refers responses of people towards their attitude in case a flexible model is available which gives them the opportunity to book tickets over a time window rather than one particular flight at a particular time. As is clearly evident even majority of the people who recorded the fact that they did not reschedule their flight even once in the last one year would prefer having such a mechanism which gives them protection from the risk of having to pay much higher if they change their mind.

 

The fourth level indicates the responses towards how much would the user be willing to pay and as per the responses majority of the people find it comfortable to cough 10% more cash on their ticket price if that cash in any way guarantees them flexibility.   

Conclusion

An add-on service like the flex flights would be a welcome initiative in the flight travel related industries. Any such add-on would include many benefits like.

 

1.Consumers are getting a peace of mind that they are safe with the dates and also they get a chance to book the flights at a cheaper rate as compared to doing it later.


2.This model will encourage early booking of tickets and companies will get more money to invest in other options than earlier. This would also ensure that while all the seats get filled early, the chances of bankruptcy would decrease.
 

3. Airline companies are getting a premium. The number of reschedulings(by the concept of flexible flights) will be higher. This will help fight companies to balance the flow of passengers per flight.
 

4. The companies should readily accept the model as this provides them some assured early bookings, premium for the seats which otherwise compromise on travel dates and at the same time gives some control over the other seats outside this quota of flex flights where they apply the normal pricing.
